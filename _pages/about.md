I am a first-year PhD student in Linguistics at Stanford University, advised by Rob Podesva. I began my scholarly work in linguistics as a B.A. student in Cognitive Science, with a Linguistics and Africana studies double minor, at Pomona College. My interdisciplinary work at Pomona has inspired me to further understand how sociolinguistics interacts with race, and cognition.

Currently, my research interests center the perception and use of African-American English on social media. My analysis considers the harmful effects of language crossing, antiblackness & hypervisibility on the internet, as well as the construction of race and gender through the use of African-American English.

Outside of the Department of Linguistics, I am a member of Dr. Anne Charity Hudley’s [BAD Lab](https://badlab.stanford.edu/), while also serving as a graduate student assistant to her [joint-project](https://charityhudleydickterfranz.com) with Dr. Hannah Franz. I am also a graduate student research affiliate of Stanford’s [SPARQ](https://sparq.stanford.edu/who-we-are/our-team).
